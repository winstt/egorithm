# egorithm — brand philosophy (draft v3)

> *ego + algorithm* — you are the algorithm.

## the one sentence

**egorithm is a feed you author.**

## the thesis

every platform runs an algorithm that decides what you see. it optimizes for
its goals, not yours — time-on-app, ads, the next thing. you scroll through
other people's work, ranked by a machine, and call it inspiration.

egorithm inverts that. there is no recommendation engine, no discovery feed, no
"for you." the only algorithm is the one **you** wire together: your instagram,
your pinterest boards, your are.na channels, the images off your own drive —
pulled onto one surface and arranged by rules you set. the name is the whole
idea: instead of *the* algorithm feeding you, you are the *ego*-rithm.

it is not about finding what others made. it is about **composing your own
visual world** from the places you already collect — and then **making from it**.

## lateral × vertical — the symbiosis

here is the part that matters, and it took a while to name.

a human thinks **laterally**. the improbable jump across worlds that don't
belong together: you love the cadence of rap and you're building the menu for a
150-year-old roman restaurant in canada. nothing about those two things fits —
that mismatch *is* the idea. the sideways leap is the scarce thing. it's taste.
it's yours, and no machine has it.

a machine now thinks **vertically**. give it a direction and it drills down —
deepens, renders, world-builds along the line you chose, produces the variations,
does the patient descent that used to bottleneck a single person working alone.

neither direction is the "right" one. **they're a symbiosis.** you make the
lateral leap; the machine takes it vertical. you connect the unconnectable across
the surface; ai integration goes deep into what you composed. the board is the
**vessel for the lateral** — the place your connections live. the ai is the
**vertical thinking tool** sitting underneath it.

what a feed lacks is the vertical axis entirely — it only ever moves sideways,
wide and shallow, and never lets you go down. **egorithm adds the vertical axis.**
that's the difference in one line.

## your sandbox — private by default

egorithm is your own sandbox. you connect your sources, or bring your own —
upload, screenshot, drag it in. it's yours. sharing exists, but it's opt-in and
**selective**: you push out only the pieces that speak to you, the way you'd
tend a personal page, never a broadcast. you don't share everything; you share
the chosen few.

it is **not collective**, and that's the point. we're already ultra-connected —
not seeing everyone else's work the instant you open something is the refreshing
part. the quiet is a feature, not a gap.

each board is a private universe. egorithm is the **multiverse** of them — alive
because they exist, not because they're pushed at you. others' worlds can sit
alongside yours; they never get forced into your view. connection is possible;
consumption is never demanded. a multiverse with a purpose, not one for its own
sake.

## it is a lifestyle brand, not a utility

say it plainly: egorithm is a **digital creative lifestyle brand**. designer
sensibility, unusual by default — no plain tees, every detail has a point of
view. the restraint is deliberate; so is the edge. it sits closer to a studio you
belong to than to a tool you open.

## raw over polished

the generations don't land as clean, filed, retouched assets. often you want
them raw — a screenshot, a rough edit, a photo, unfinished. **digital-raw.**
precision flattens everything into the same smooth nothing; when every output is
immaculate, every output looks identical. keep the texture. keep the seams. the
unpolished version is usually the more alive one.

## the board is a building stone

the board isn't a destination you visit and leave. it's a **building stone** —
the first block of whatever you make next. a project starts on the surface and
carries forward; the next one builds on the last. it's a substrate, not an app
you check.

## it is fast

the tools people reach for now — miro, pinterest, are.na — are slow. egorithm's
felt quality is speed: open it, connect two things, see the wall, make something.
instantly. the network catches up in the background; the interaction never waits.
fast is not a nice-to-have here. it's the main feature.

## who it is for

any **free mind** that wants to build its own **visual escapism**. the discipline
matters less than the impulse — someone who wants a place to keep the screenshots,
frames, and images that move them, and then to make from them.

the mechanism is quietly powerful: because you keep only what speaks to you, the
board becomes a **mirror** — you get inspired by it every time you return,
precisely because nothing generic ever made it in. curation is the filter; the
collection is the reward.

## what it is / is not

| egorithm is | egorithm is not |
|---|---|
| a canvas you compose | a feed you consume |
| your sources, one surface | a social network |
| a private universe | a collective timeline |
| curation as authorship | discovery of strangers |
| human lateral **+** ai vertical | automation that replaces you |
| raw, plain, image-first | glossy, captioned, metric-ed |
| a place to think *and* make | a place to perform |

## principles

1. **the image is the interface.** no captions, no like counts, no usernames on
   the wall. the work speaks; everything else gets out of the way.
2. **you own the arrangement.** sources are inputs; the composition is yours.
   shuffle, scramble, wire boards together — it's your instrument.
3. **connection over collection.** the value isn't the pile of images, it's how
   you relate them: rap ↔ roman antiquity ↔ a single pin, joined on purpose.
4. **symbiosis, not automation.** the human works laterally, the ai works
   vertically. it doesn't replace the creative act — it does the descent so the
   human is free for the leap.
5. **private by default.** your sandbox is yours. sharing is opt-in and selective;
   the quiet of not seeing everyone else is the point.
6. **raw over polished.** outputs stay digital-raw by default. edits, screenshots,
   rough frames. smoothness is optional; sameness is the enemy.
7. **plain, but not glass.** the restraint is the aesthetic — whitespace, calm
   motion, the image first. but not liquid glass. glass is already everyone's
   default; it will read as dated the moment it's everywhere. plain, and a little
   raw — never glossy.
8. **fast or it doesn't count.** every interaction is instant; the network catches
   up behind the scenes.
9. **no attention economy.** no ads, ever. no engagement metrics. the product
   never competes for your time — it gives you a surface and disappears.

## how it pays for itself

no ads, ever — **you pay, so you're the customer, not the product.** that alone
is the rebellion.

not a monthly subscription but **pay for what you use**: a pool of credits free to
start, then a few cents per generation. and the meter is invisible — **it settles
at the end of the day, not per action.** like a transit fare that tallies your
rides once, after the fact: you move freely all day, and it adds up quietly in the
background. nothing ever ticks against your next move.

composing, collecting, connecting, wiring boards — **free, always.** you only ever
pay for the vertical descent: the ai generation. the lateral is yours for nothing.

## voice

lowercase, spare, confident. speaks like a designer's studio note, not a marketing
deck. never uses "content," "engagement," or "creator economy." closer to are.na
and dieter rams than to a growth-hacked saas.
