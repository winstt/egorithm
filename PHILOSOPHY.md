# EGORITHM — Brand Philosophy (draft)

> *ego + algorithm* — you are the algorithm.

## The one sentence

**EGORITHM is a feed you author.**

## The thesis

Every platform runs an algorithm that decides what you see. It optimizes for
its goals, not yours — engagement, time-on-app, ads. You scroll through other
people's work, ranked by a machine, and call it inspiration.

EGORITHM inverts that. There is no recommendation engine, no discovery feed, no
"for you." The only algorithm is the one **you** wire together: your Instagram,
your Pinterest boards, your Are.na channels, the images off your own drive —
pulled into one infinite surface and arranged by rules you set. The name is the
whole idea: instead of *the* algorithm feeding you, you are the *ego*-rithm.

It is not about finding what others made. It is about **composing your own
visual world** from the places you already collect — and then **making from it**.
Select a few images, push them through an AI visual engine, and create. The
board isn't a gallery to look at; it's a **device for thinking vertically** —
going deeper into an idea, not wider across a feed.

And it is **fast**. The tools people reach for now — Miro, Pinterest, Are.na —
are slow. EGORITHM's felt quality is speed: open it, connect two things, see the
wall, make something. Instantly.

## What it is / is not

| EGORITHM is | EGORITHM is not |
|---|---|
| A canvas you compose | A feed you consume |
| Your sources, connected | A social network |
| Curation as authorship | Discovery of strangers |
| Quiet, plain, image-first | Chrome, captions, metrics |
| A place to *think visually* | A place to perform |

## Principles

1. **The image is the interface.** No captions, no like counts, no usernames on
   the wall. The work speaks; everything else gets out of the way.
2. **You own the arrangement.** Sources are inputs; the composition is yours.
   Shuffle, scramble, wire boards together — it's your instrument.
3. **Connection over collection.** The value isn't the pile of images, it's how
   you relate them: architecture ↔ cats ↔ a single art pin, joined on purpose.
4. **No attention economy.** No ads, ever. No engagement metrics. The product
   never competes for your time — it gives you a surface and disappears.
5. **Plain by conviction.** The restraint is the aesthetic. Whitespace, one
   glass control, smooth motion. Calm software.
6. **Fast or it doesn't count.** Speed is a feature, the main one. Every
   interaction is instant; the network catches up in the background.
7. **Look, then make.** The board is raw material. Selecting images and
   generating from them turns collecting into creating — a tool for vertical
   thinking, not just viewing.

## Voice

Lowercase, spare, confident. Speaks like a designer's studio note, not a
marketing deck. Never uses "content," "engagement," or "creator economy."
Closer to Are.na and Dieter Rams than to a growth-hacked SaaS.
